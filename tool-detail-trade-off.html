THE TEST

The Trade-Off

Force trade-offs to reveal what customers actually value.

Forces you to build competing versions of your product and choose between them as your customer would. Each round requires a sacrifice. Features that survive every trade-off are the core value.

When to use it

Use The Trade-Off when your feature-heavy product can't prioritise, when you're preparing an MVP launch and need to know what to ship first, or when the team is debating feature cuts and nobody wants to let go.

It's particularly effective for pricing strategy work — understanding what customers will actually pay for — and before user research, when you need sharper hypotheses about what matters most.

How it works in The Studio

Here's how a session works with Pete:

1
Define the Offer
Pete helps you list the 5-7 dimensions of your product or service — features, price points, support levels, delivery speed, and anything else your customer cares about.
2
The Trade-Offs
Over 6-8 forced-choice rounds, Pete presents two competing versions of your offer. You must choose one — no "both." Each round forces a sacrifice.
3
The Value Stack
Pete ranks your features by survival rate — how often each one was kept when something else had to go. The pattern reveals your customer's true priorities.
4
Minimum Viable Offer
Together you define the leanest version of your product that still delivers the core value — the features that won every round, at a price point that matches.

Your downloadable report includes the full trade-off rounds, the Value Stack ranking, Pete's coaching observations on your decision patterns, and relevant Wade articles and programs.

Foundation

Rooted in conjoint analysis developed by Luce & Tukey (1964) and extended by Green & Srinivasan (1978). The method underpins willingness-to-pay research across consumer and B2B markets. Used at scale by Sawtooth Software, Procter & Gamble, Apple, and Amazon to design products and set prices.

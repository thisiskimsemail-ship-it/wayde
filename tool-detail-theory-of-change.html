Theory of Change

Map the causal chain from what you do to the change you create.

Works backward from the long-term change you want to create, mapping every condition that has to be true for it to happen. Exposes the "missing middle" between activities and outcomes. Makes implicit causal logic explicit.

When to use it

Use Theory of Change when your success depends on things beyond your direct control — behaviour change, ecosystem development, systemic shifts, or multi-stakeholder adoption.

Social entrepreneurs articulating impact for funders. Innovation leads whose success depends on systemic change. Founders whose business model assumes behaviour change or ecosystem development. Grant applicants needing a logic model. Anyone with a "missing middle" between what they do and what they hope happens.

How it works in The Studio

Here's how a session works with Pete:

1
Define the Outcome
"If everything works perfectly over 5 years, what has changed? For whom?" Pete pushes past product descriptions to observable impact.
2
Map the Preconditions
Work backwards: "For that to happen, what has to be true?" Build a chain of conditions from present to outcome.
3
Sort by Sphere of Influence
Classify every precondition: Within Control (green), Within Influence (amber), or Outside Control (red).
4
Design the Activities
For controllable preconditions, design specific activities. Test the connection between what you do and what it creates.
5
Test the Logic
Read the full chain back. Identify the weakest link. Name critical assumptions. "What's the simplest test for the weakest link?"

Sample output

Here's what a Theory of Change pathway looks like in practice:

Example: Education Platform Impact
OUTCOME
Teachers in under-resourced schools spend 30% less time on admin, 30% more on instruction
PRECONDITION — OUTSIDE CONTROL
Regulatory body updates accreditation requirements
PRECONDITION — OUTSIDE CONTROL
School administrators grant time for professional development
PRECONDITION — WITHIN CONTROL
Teachers are trained and confident using the platform
PRECONDITION — WITHIN CONTROL
Platform adopted by pilot schools
ACTIVITIES
Build platform, create training programme, recruit pilot schools, run workshops
Weakest Link
"Admin grants time for professional development" — outside your control, blocks the entire chain.
TEST
Interview 5 school administrators this month to validate willingness.

What you get

A complete causal pathway from activities to impact, every precondition rated by controllability, critical external assumptions identified, the weakest link highlighted, and a concrete test to validate it.

Your downloadable report includes the full pathway map, Pete's coaching observations, sphere-of-influence ratings, and a recommended first step.

Foundation

Originated with Carol Weiss at Harvard ("Nothing as Practical as Good Theory," 1995). Developed through the Aspen Institute Roundtable on Community Change. Widely adopted by the Gates Foundation, DFID, USAID, and ActKnowledge. Used across philanthropy, international development, social enterprise, and increasingly in corporate innovation.

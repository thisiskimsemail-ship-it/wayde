You came in with a home battery system built around four dimensions — storage capacity, warranty, backup speed, and smart features — each with a basic and premium tier. “The AI grid trading is what sets us apart,” you said. Six rounds of forced trade-offs later, the picture looks very different.

Two dimensions dominate. One is a conditional nice-to-have. And the feature you spent the longest building — AI load optimisation and grid trading, the centrepiece of every investor pitch — won just one of six rounds.

The challenge

Launching a residential battery storage system into a market dominated by Tesla Powerwall. Four product dimensions, each with a Low and High level, priced between $5,900 and $13,900 installed. The question isn't which features to build — it's which combination a homeowner would actually choose over the incumbent.

The first $4,000 — from $5,900 to $9,900 — buys a 13 kWh battery and 15-year warranty: the two dimensions that won almost every round. That jump delivers 73% of total customer value.

The next $2,000 — $9,900 to $11,900 — adds instant backup, a dimension that won half its rounds. Defensible upsell for homeowners in blackout-prone areas.

The cliff: the final $2,000 — $11,900 to $13,900 — buys AI grid trading, which won one round out of six. That's $2,000 for a feature homeowners actively traded away. The pricing ceiling is $11,900.

The reframe

Before: “We need AI grid trading and instant switchover to compete with Tesla Powerwall.”

After: “We need a 13 kWh battery with a 15-year warranty at $9,900 — and we need to be the brand homeowners trust to keep the lights on.”

Questions worth sitting with

Product

Is the 13 kWh unit genuinely reliable enough to back with a 15-year guarantee, or does the hardware need more investment?

Pricing

Can you defend the $2,000 jump to $11,900 with a clear instant-backup demo for blackout-prone areas?

Honesty

Is the AI grid trading still on the roadmap because customers want it, or because your engineers do?

Competition

If a competitor launched a 13 kWh unit with a 15-year warranty at $7,900, how would you respond?

What to do next

  1. Launch with the $9,900 MVO. 13 kWh storage and 15-year warranty. Basic app. 30-second switchover. Get it installed.
  2. Test the $11,900 upsell. Show 10 homeowners in storm-prone areas the instant backup demo. Measure willingness to pay $2,000 more.
  3. Rewrite positioning: lead with reliability. “13 kWh. 15 years. Your home stays on.” is the headline. Not “AI-powered energy intelligence.”
  4. Shelf the AI grid trading. Don't delete the code — remove it from product and marketing until 100 installed customers ask for it.

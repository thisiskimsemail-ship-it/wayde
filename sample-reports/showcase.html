You came in with seven features planned for launch. A full-featured project management tool for mid-size creative agencies. “We need all of them,” you said. Six rounds of forced trade-offs later, the picture looks very different.

Three features dominate. Three are expendable. And the one you were most excited about — real-time collaboration, the centrepiece of the pitch deck, the most technically complex thing you've built — lost five of six rounds.

The challenge

Building a project management SaaS for mid-size agencies (15–80 people). Seven features in the launch roadmap, limited runway, and a team convinced everything is equally important. The real question: if you can only ship three features at launch, which three?

Key moments

These were the statements that shifted the conversation:

The pattern emerges“I keep choosing reporting over collaboration — I think our customers don't care about what we care about.” The first sign that builder excitement and buyer need had diverged.
The reframe“Automated reporting saves an agency owner 45 minutes every Monday. Collaboration saves us a cool demo.” Reframed the entire value proposition from novelty to utility.
The admission“If I'm honest, we built collaboration because it was fun to build. Not because anyone asked for it.” The admission that changed the launch plan.

The reframe

Before: “We need to ship all seven features to compete with Monday.com and Asana.”

After: “We need to ship three features that save an agency owner 45 minutes every week — and charge less than the tools that try to do everything.”

The minimum viable offer is simpler and cheaper than planned — but that's a faster path to revenue and a clearer positioning story. Lead with utility, not novelty. The features that lost aren't dead ideas — they're roadmap items for after you have 50 paying customers.

Questions worth sitting with

PRODUCT

Is the reporting engine genuinely best-in-class, or an afterthought that now needs real investment?

POSITIONING

What does the pricing page look like leading with “Automated client reports in 30 seconds” instead of “All-in-one project management”?

HONESTY

Which expendable features are you still emotionally attached to — and why?

COMPETITION

If a competitor launched with just reporting + integrations tomorrow, how would you respond?

What to do next

  1. Rebuild launch scope around the three must-haves. Reporting, integrations, time tracking. Cut everything else from v1. Ship faster, learn faster.
  2. Run 5 customer interviews showing two pricing tiers — core bundle at $39/mo vs. full suite at $69/mo. Let real agency owners decide.
  3. Rewrite positioning: lead with the boring feature. “Automated client reports in 30 seconds” is the headline. Boring to you. Valuable to them.
  4. Shelf the collaboration engine. Don't delete the code — remove it from product and marketing until 50 paying customers ask for it.

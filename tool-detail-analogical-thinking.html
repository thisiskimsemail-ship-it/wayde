THE SPARK

Mash Up

Smash two worlds together. See what comes out.

Most ideation tools help people generate ideas from their own experience. The problem is that experience has edges. Mash Up is systematic cross-pollination with energy. Pete pulls in unexpected domains, collides them with your challenge in real time, and asks what you hear in the collision. Some combinations are duds. Some produce something genuinely new. The speed and volume is the point.

When to use it

Use when conventional approaches keep producing similar ideas, when the team is stuck in its own industry bubble, or when you want a genuinely differentiated solution. A logistics founder thinks about logistics solutions. A health-tech CEO thinks about health-tech solutions. The most valuable ideas often come from a completely different domain that solved a structurally similar problem years ago.

Core tool in The Spark for breaking out of familiar thinking.

How it works in The Studio

Here's how a session works with Pete:

1
Define the core challenge
What specific problem needs solving? Pete gets you to a clean problem statement.
2
Abstract the structure
Strip away the context. What type of problem is this at its core? This is the unlock.
3
Collision round
Pete fires domains at you — nature, military, hospitality, sport, music — and asks what you hear in each collision. Rate each: hot, warm, or cold.
4
Pull the hot ones forward
The collisions that resonated get examined. What's the transferable mechanism? Why did it land?
5
Remix into your context
Take the best mechanisms and adapt them into concrete ideas for your challenge.
6
Pick your first experiment
Which remixed idea do you prototype first? What's the smallest version you could test this week?

Sample output

Here's what a Mash Up session board looks like:

Example: Reducing Customer Support Wait Times
Problem
Customers wait too long for support, then leave.
Abstract Structure
Queue management under variable demand.
How do you serve people faster without adding capacity?
Restaurants
Appetisers while you wait — the wait becomes part of the experience.
Mechanism: Fill dead time with value so the queue feels shorter.
★ Hot
Healthcare
Emergency room triage — don't serve in order, serve by urgency.
Mechanism: Priority-based routing by severity.
Warm
Airlines
Self-service kiosks handle 80% of check-ins without a human.
Mechanism: Automate simple, reserve humans for complex.
Cold
Remixed Ideas
From: Restaurants
Serve quick-win content while customers are in queue — relevant help articles, video walkthroughs, community answers. If 40% of issues resolve before an agent connects, queue length drops without adding headcount.
Test: Add a contextual help carousel to the waiting screen this sprint.
From: Healthcare
Route by issue severity, not arrival time. VIP and revenue-critical tickets jump the queue; simple queries get instant bot resolution.
Test: Tag top 3 ticket categories by priority and measure resolution time change.

What you get

A set of collision cards from unexpected domains, each revealing a transferable mechanism. The session ends with remixed ideas adapted to your challenge, each tagged back to its source analogy, plus a concrete first experiment to run.

Foundation

Rooted in IDEO's Mash Up methodology and cross-pollination practice. Draws on the same intellectual territory as classical analogical reasoning (Aristotle), biomimicry, and DARPA's structured analogy programs. Used by P&G's Connect + Develop program to source external innovations.

THE TEST

Reality Check

Confront the gap between your story and your data.

Pete asks for the receipts. For every claim you make about your business or project, Pete asks: what's the actual data? When the story and the numbers don't match, that's the insight. Not an attack on the narrative — a confrontation with evidence.

When to use it

Use Reality Check when you've been telling the same growth story for months and haven't tested it against data. When you're preparing for a board or investor update. At a project midpoint where an honest assessment matters more than optimism.

It's particularly effective for anyone who suspects a gap between what they're saying and what's actually real.

How it works in The Studio

Here's how a session works with Pete:

1
What's Your Story?
"Tell me how things are going. Not the investor version." Pete captures every claim — without challenging yet.
2
Show Me the Evidence
For every claim, Pete asks for actual data. "You said growth is strong. What's the number? Over what period?"
3
Where's the Gap?
Pete reflects back the delta between story and data. Claims tagged: Supported (green), Partial (amber), Unsupported (red).
4
The Honest Picture
Rewrite the narrative to match reality. Identify 3 key metrics to watch. "What's one thing you'll do this week?"

Sample output

UNSUPPORTED
Revised Narrative
We have early traction with a product that some users value, but retention is a serious problem. Net customer growth is near zero. The team needs attention before we can scale anything.
3 Key Metrics
Monthly Retention Rate Net Customer Growth Team Engagement Score

What you get

An evidence-rated narrative audit — every claim you make paired with the data that supports (or doesn't support) it. A gap analysis showing where story and reality diverge. A revised honest narrative. And 3 key metrics to actually watch going forward.

Your downloadable report includes the full claim-evidence audit, Pete's coaching observations, your revised narrative, and relevant Wade articles and programs.

Built on Andy Grove's "Only the Paranoid Survive" (1996), Ray Dalio's "Principles" (2017) and Bridgewater's radical transparency culture, Ben Horowitz's "The Hard Thing About Hard Things" (2014), and the scientific method tradition of hypothesis testing against evidence.

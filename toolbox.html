Elevator Pitch Builder

~5-10 min in The Studio

Articulate your idea in a single clear sentence using a structured mad-lib framework. For anyone pitching something new — whether that's a startup, a corporate initiative, or a social enterprise.

The framework

For [target customer] who [problem/need], [product/service] is a [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [alternatives], we [differentiator].

When to use

When you can't yet explain your idea clearly, when you're short on time, when you need to pitch to leadership, or when you want to sharpen your one-liner before going deeper with a Lean Canvas.

What you get

A tight, tested pitch sentence — plus a strength check on each component. If you want to go deeper, your pitch carries straight into a Lean Canvas session.

Lean Canvas

~20 min in The Studio

A one-page business model that maps the 9 key assumptions behind any venture, product, or initiative. Created by Ash Maurya, adapted from Alexander Osterwalder's Business Model Canvas. Wade uses it across multiple programs because it forces you to get concrete about what you're really betting on.

The 9 blocks

Customer Segments — Who are you creating value for?
Problem — What are the top 1-3 problems they face?
Unique Value Proposition — Why are you different and worth paying attention to?
Solution — How do you solve each problem?
Channels — How do you reach your customers?
Revenue Streams — How do you make money?
Cost Structure — What are your key costs?
Key Metrics — What numbers tell you the business is working?
Unfair Advantage — What can't be easily copied or bought?

When to use

When you're developing a new business model, product, or initiative and need to surface your riskiest assumptions before investing time and money. Works for startups, corporate innovation projects, and investment thesis development.

What you get

A completed canvas with your key hypotheses mapped, your riskiest assumption identified, and a concrete next step to test it. In The Studio, Pete guides you through each block conversationally and builds your canvas in real time.

Where it comes from

Ash Maurya's Running Lean (2012). Adapted from Alex Osterwalder's Business Model Canvas. Used by Charlie Simpson in Your Growth Engine, Brian Collins in Think Like an Entrepreneur, and Sally Bruce in The AI Conundrum at Wade Institute.

The Toolbox

Each tool is a structured exercise that Pete walks you through. They take 15–30 minutes and produce a real output you can use. Click any tool to learn more, or jump straight in.

THE UNTANGLE Understand the real problem
Five Whys
Ask "why?" five times to peel back symptoms and find the root cause. Based on Toyota's production system — now used at Amazon, GE, and Harvard Business School.
~15 minOutput: Root cause statement + systemic change
Empathy Map
Map what your user says, thinks, does, and feels. The power is in the gaps — where what someone says contradicts what they do. That's where the real insight lives.
~20 minOutput: User profile + key insight
Jobs to Be Done
People don't buy products — they hire them to make progress. Uncover the functional, emotional, and social job your customer is really trying to get done.
~20 minOutput: Job statement + competitive landscape
THE SPARK Explore and generate ideas
Crazy 8s
Eight ideas in eight minutes. Time pressure breaks perfectionism and pushes you past the safe, obvious answers. From Google Ventures' Design Sprint.
~15 minOutput: 8 ideas + top pick
How Might We
Convert problems into open-ended design questions. "How" implies solvable, "might" means possibility, "we" signals collaboration. Each question opens a different direction.
~20 minOutput: 5–8 reframed questions
SCAMPER
Seven creative lenses: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse. Each one forces your brain out of its default groove.
~20 minOutput: Ideas from 7 angles
THE TEST Stress-test before you commit
Pre-Mortem
Imagine the project has already failed. Work backwards to identify why. Prospective hindsight bypasses optimism bias and surfaces risks nobody would say out loud.
~20 minOutput: Risk register + mitigations
Devil's Advocate
Pete argues the strongest possible case against your idea. Not to kill it — to force it to become better. If it survives Pete, it'll survive the market.
~25 minOutput: Objections + verdict
Analogical Thinking
Borrow solutions from unexpected places. The Shinkansen nose came from a kingfisher's beak. Velcro from burs. Most problems have been solved somewhere else.
~20 minOutput: 3 analogies + application
THE BUILD Turn ideas into plans
Lean Canvas
Nine blocks on one page: Problem, Customer, Value Prop, Solution, Channels, Revenue, Costs, Metrics, Unfair Advantage. Start with the problem, not the solution.
~30 minOutput: One-page business model
Effectuation
Start with who you are, what you know, and who you know — then figure out what you can build from that. How expert entrepreneurs actually think, based on research by Saras Sarasvathy.
~20 minOutput: Means inventory + first move
Rapid Experiment
Design the cheapest, fastest test that could disprove your riskiest assumption. From the Lean Startup's Build-Measure-Learn loop. Goal: a decision, not proof.
~20 minOutput: Experiment design + pass/fail criteria

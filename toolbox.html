Wade Innovation Toolbox

12 tools across 4 stages. Pete recommends the right tool for you — or pick one yourself.

Clarify Understand the problem before solving it.

Five Whys

15 min

Ask "why?" five times to move past symptoms and find the root cause. From Toyota's production system. Are you sure you understand the problem?

Try Five Whys →

Jobs to Be Done

20 min

Discover what your customer is really hiring your product to do. Based on Clayton Christensen's framework — the job, not the feature, is what matters.

Try Jobs to Be Done →

Empathy Map

20 min

Step into your user's world. Map what they think, feel, say, and do — then find the gaps between them. A human-centred design essential.

Try Empathy Map →
Ideate Generate possibilities before narrowing focus.

Crazy 8s

15 min

Generate 8 distinct ideas fast. From Google's Design Sprint. Forces you past the obvious first idea into genuinely creative territory.

Try Crazy 8s →

How Might We

20 min

Reframe your problem from multiple angles using "How might we..." statements, then brainstorm solutions. Stanford d.school's bridge between problem and solution.

Try How Might We →

SCAMPER

20 min

Stretch an existing idea through seven creative lenses: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other use, Eliminate, Reverse. Systematic creativity.

Try SCAMPER →
Validate Test your thinking before committing resources.

Pre-Mortem

20 min

Imagine your idea has failed completely. Work backwards to surface the risks you'd miss from an optimistic mindset. By psychologist Gary Klein.

Try Pre-Mortem →

Devil's Advocate

25 min

Pete actively argues against your idea across five dimensions. The toughest challenge in the toolbox. Like a sceptical investor Q&A in a safe environment.

Try Devil's Advocate →

Rapid Experiment

15 min

Design the cheapest, fastest test for your riskiest assumption. Based on Lean Startup's Build-Measure-Learn loop. Test before you build.

Try Rapid Experiment →
Develop Turn insight into action.

Lean Canvas

25 min

Map the full business model on one page. 9 blocks covering customers, problem, solution, channels, revenue, costs, metrics, and unfair advantage. By Ash Maurya.

Try Lean Canvas →

Effectuation

20 min

Start with what you have, not what you need. Professor Saras Sarasvathy's framework for entrepreneurial action. What's possible from your skills, network, and resources right now?

Try Effectuation →

Analogical Thinking

20 min

Borrow solutions from unexpected places. Find proven patterns from other industries, domains, or disciplines and adapt them to your challenge.

Try Analogical Thinking →

Wardley Mapping

Map your value chain from user need to components.

A Wardley Map plots every component of your value chain on two axes: visibility (how close to the user) and evolution (how mature/commoditised). Unlike business model canvases that describe what you do, a Wardley Map reveals where you are — and where things are moving. It exposes which parts of your value chain are truly differentiating and which are becoming commodities.

When to use it

Use Wardley Mapping when you're making build-vs-buy decisions, when you're not sure where your competitive advantage actually lives, or when your team is debating where to invest engineering time. It's particularly effective after a Lean Canvas session when you want to understand the strategic landscape underneath your business model, or when you need to explain your position to investors.

How it works in The Studio

Here's how a session works with Pete:

1
Anchor to the User Need
"What does the user actually need to accomplish?" Pete helps you define the anchor need and walk down the value chain from visible need to deep infrastructure.
2
Map the Components
Surface 8–15 components that must exist to serve the user need. From the UI layer down to data, infrastructure, and dependencies.
3
Plot Evolution
For each component, determine: Genesis (novel), Custom (bespoke), Product (off-the-shelf), or Commodity (utility). Pete challenges mismatches.
4
Find Strategic Plays
Identify mismatches (building what you should buy), inertia (resisting commoditisation), opportunities (genesis components that could be a moat), and dependency risks.
5
The Strategic Decision
Synthesise into clear build/buy/partner recommendations. Three moves: stop building X, double down on Y, watch Z.

What you get

A value chain map with evolution stages plotted for every component. Strategic mismatches identified — where you're building what you should buy, or buying what should be your differentiator.

Clear build/buy/partner recommendations and a landscape view that competitors can't see. Your downloadable report includes the full map, Pete's coaching observations on strategic positioning, and relevant Wade articles and programs.

Foundation

Developed by Simon Wardley in 2005 while serving as CEO of Fotango. Wardley Mapping has since been adopted widely in technology strategy and digital transformation. Used by governments, startups, and enterprises to visualise strategic positioning and make better build-vs-buy decisions.

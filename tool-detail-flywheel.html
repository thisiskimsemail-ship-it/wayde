Flywheel

Map the reinforcing loop that drives your growth.

A flywheel is a reinforcing loop — a set of 3–5 activities where each one feeds the next and the whole system accelerates over time. Most people understand the idea but very few can articulate their own flywheel clearly. This tool helps you find the real loop and identify the weakest link limiting your momentum.

When to use it

Use the Flywheel when you have a product or business running but growth has stalled or feels random. It's for when you can describe what you do but not why it compounds — or when your team can't agree on what actually drives growth.

It's particularly effective after you've completed a Lean Canvas and want to understand the growth engine underneath it, or when you need to show a board or investors how your initiative will compound, not just launch.

How it works in The Studio

Here's how a session works with Pete:

1
Find the Engine
"What's the one thing that, when it works, makes everything else easier?" Pete helps you surface 3–5 components and compress ruthlessly — more than 5 means weak connections.
2
Test the Connections
Walk through each link. "Does A lead to B automatically, or does something trigger it?" Label each connection: Strong (proven), Developing (early signals), or Unproven (assumed).
3
Find the Bottleneck
"If you could only strengthen one connection, which unlocks the most momentum?" Pete pushes past the obvious answer to find the real constraint.
4
The Acceleration Question
"What would it take to make that connection twice as strong in 90 days?" Then: "What's one thing you'll do in the next 48 hours?"

Sample output

Here's what a flywheel diagram looks like in practice:

Example: Media Company Growth Flywheel
COMPONENT 1
Great Content
Leads to Audience Growth STRONG
COMPONENT 2
Audience Growth
Leads to Advertiser Demand UNPROVEN
COMPONENT 3
Advertiser Demand
Leads to Revenue for Reinvestment DEVELOPING
COMPONENT 4
Revenue for Reinvestment
Leads to Great Content STRONG
Bottleneck
Audience Growth → Advertiser Demand (UNPROVEN) — no evidence yet that audience size drives advertiser willingness to pay.
Acceleration Action
Run 3 sponsored content tests this month to prove advertiser demand correlates with audience size. First step in 48 hours: brief the sales team on three target advertisers.

What you get

A tight flywheel diagram with 3–5 components, each connection annotated with its mechanism and strength (Strong / Developing / Unproven). The bottleneck is identified along with a concrete 90-day acceleration plan and a 48-hour first step.

Your downloadable report includes the full flywheel map, Pete's coaching observations on where the loop is weakest, a recommended next step, and relevant Wade articles and programs.

Foundation

Developed by Jim Collins in "Good to Great" (2001). Made famous by Jeff Bezos at Amazon with the most well-known business flywheel. Popularised as a replacement for the sales funnel by Brian Halligan and Dharmesh Shah at HubSpot.

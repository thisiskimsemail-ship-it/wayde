Lean Canvas

Outline your venture model on a single page.

One-page business model framework with nine blocks: Problem, Customer Segments, Unique Value Proposition, Solution, Channels, Revenue Streams, Cost Structure, Key Metrics, Unfair Advantage. Designed to be completed in under 30 minutes, updated frequently. Start with the problem, not the solution.

Quick Facts
Duration~30 minutes

When to use it

Use at the early stage of a venture or new initiative when you want assumptions explicit and to test the riskiest ones first. Also for re-evaluating when something isn't working.

Core tool in The Build when you're ready to turn an idea into a model.

Sample output

Here's what a Lean Canvas looks like in practice:

Example: EdTech Startup
Problem
Teachers spend 5+ hrs/week on admin, not teaching
Solution
AI-powered lesson planning + automated parent communication
UVP
"Save 5 hours a week — we handle the admin so you can teach"
Customer
K-8 teachers in independent schools
Channels
Teacher conferences, word-of-mouth, education podcasts
Revenue
$15/month per teacher, school-wide licensing
Costs
Engineering team, cloud hosting, education content
Metrics
Weekly active teachers, lesson plans generated, school retention
Unfair Advantage ★
Partnership with 3 school networks (800 schools)
Riskiest Assumption
"Teachers will trust AI-generated lesson plans." If they don't, nothing else on the canvas matters. Test this first — not the product, not the pricing, not the channel.

What you get

Completed one-page canvas making every key assumption visible, identifying the riskiest assumption to test first. Not a plan — a hypothesis.

Foundation

Created by Ash Maurya in "Running Lean" (2012). Adapted from Alexander Osterwalder's Business Model Canvas. Used by Techstars, 500 Startups, MIT Sloan, Stanford GSB, INSEAD, Intuit, Adobe, and Atlassian.

SCAMPER

Stretch an idea by substituting, combining, adapting and more.

Structured ideation checklist with seven lenses: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify/Magnify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse/Rearrange. Each lens forces the mind out of default mode into a new angle.

Quick Facts
Duration~20 minutes
CategoryThe Spark
OriginBuilding on Osborn, 1971
Created byBob Eberle

When to use it

Use when brainstorming feels repetitive, when improving an existing product, service, or process rather than creating something new, or when teams find open-ended ideation unfocused.

Great in The Spark when you have something concrete to remix.

How it works in The Studio

Pete guides you through a structured conversation. Here's what to expect:

What you get

Broad set of raw ideas from seven distinct angles. Goal is volume and variety. Best ideas from each lens selected for development or testing.

Foundation

Created by Bob Eberle in 1971, building on Alex Osborn's brainstorming work. Used by IDEO, INSEAD, Babson College, Philips, and 3M.

Pete's note

"Most people love Combine and hate Eliminate. That's exactly why Eliminate usually produces the best ideas — removing things is harder than adding them."
